E-SPORTS The Pinnacle of Competitive Gaming

Intro

While gaming may simply be a hobby for some, it has become the livelihood of a select few. Those who are dedicated and talented at a competitive game they play are given the opportunity to do what seemed impossible 50 years ago: play video games for a living. This page will briefly cover how the concept of ‘E-Sports’ began, and how it is slowly evolving into becoming a more mainstream form of entertainment, comparable to the likes of traditional sports like Football or Basketball.

E-Sports Today

  • 1972

    First Gaming Competition

    A small competition was organised at the Stanford University for a game called Spacewar (released in 1962)

  • 1980

    Gaming Competition attracts 10,000

    Video game industry giant at the time, Atari, organises a Space Invaders Champtionship, which had 10,000 participants.

  • 1980

    Twin Galaxies Founded

    Twin galaxies was established, moderating and giving rise to speedrunning, a form of competition where players sought to complete games as fast as possible.

  • 1990

    First Nintendo World Championships

    Nintendo World Championships was a special cartridge with pre-existing games altered for the competition (Super Mario Bros, Tetris, Rad Racer). Players competed for high scores in several games.

  • 1996

    Release of Quake

    It was a competitive first-person shooter, and online multiplayer was supported, giving the opportunity for players to finally compete in real time.

  • 1997

    Quake Competition Organised

    Over 2000 players participated in one versus one matches, with Dennis Fong emerging as the champion.

  • 1997-1999

    Starcraft Gains Traction

    The video game Starcraft explodes in popularity in South Korea, leading to numerous competitions and leading the charge in the E-Sports scene. South Korea is still a dominant force in numerous E-Sports today.

  • 2011

    Launch of Twitch.tv

    The release of Twitch, a streaming platform, made viewing gaming competitions easy for the average person, further boosting their popularity. Twitch would go on to inspire other streaming services, but is still the most widely used one today.

Challenges of Competing

While playing games professionally is becoming an increasingly enticing offer for many, often times media fails to portray the dark side of this industry.

Grueling Schedules

Just like traditional sports athletes, professional video games player are subject to grueling training schedules too. Furthermore, these schedules may be further extended when the team is not performing up to their desired standards. For example, in the Overwatch League, the Shanghai Dragons went on a historic 42 game losing streak, and it was revealed that their training regimen began at 10:30am every day and concluded at midnight. Despite their long training hours, their losing streak persisted, and it may have led to an even more intensive schedule than what was revealed by the team themselves.

Lack of Tournaments

In certain E-Sports, the company in charge of the game is not actually actively involved in the E-Sports scene. Hence, the responsibility falls to external companies to organise tournaments to allow the game's best players to compete. This leads to the glaring issue, however, that when the companies have little incentive to organise said tournaments, the players suffer as well. No tournaments means no prize money, meaning little to no income for the players' teams, which trickles down to the players themselves. This predicament is slowly taking hold of the Counter Strike: Global Offensive scene, where there is a distinct lack of tournaments in the North American region.

Mental Health

Whether it is continuous poor performances in matches, the game developer changing the game nagatively, or simply just losing interest in the game, there are a multitude of reasons for players to become jaded to the games they have dedicated their lives to. Mental health issues plague E-Sports of all types, as players are under the constant pressure to perform and neglect healthy lifestyle choices as a result. These issues, left unchecked, may increase in severity, and eventually lead to a premature conclusion of what could have been an otherwise promising career.

Conclusion

It is clear that E-Sports as an entertainment medium is going to continue to grow well into the future. As long as there is a healthy supply of new competitive games, gamers all over the world will not pass up the opportunity to test their skills at the highest level. That being said, competing at the highest level is never easy, and great thought and consideration must be put in before making the decision to pursue this exciting career path.
